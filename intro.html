Evolution of Computing

Introduction to quantum computing

Course Description

The Introduction to Quantum Computing course offers a foundational journey into one of the most transformative fields in modern technology. Blending physics, computer science, and mathematics, this course unpacks the principles of quantum mechanics and how they’re harnessed to perform computation in fundamentally new ways.

Students will explore the strange yet powerful concepts that underpin quantum systems — from superposition and entanglement to quantum gates and algorithms. The course demystifies how quantum computers differ from classical machines, what problems they’re uniquely suited to solve, and why this emerging field could redefine everything from cryptography to materials science.

Why This Course Matters

  • Builds essential literacy in a groundbreaking and rapidly evolving field
  • Introduces the core ideas behind quantum mechanics and quantum logic
  • Prepares students for advanced study or careers in quantum technologies
  • Equips learners to critically evaluate real-world quantum applications

Course Objectives

  • Understand the basic principles of quantum mechanics as applied to computation
  • Distinguish between classical and quantum computing models
  • Describe quantum bits (qubits), gates, circuits, and measurement
  • Explore landmark quantum algorithms like Shor’s and Grover’s
  • Analyze the potential and limitations of current quantum hardware
  • Discuss the societal, ethical, and security implications of quantum technology
Evolution of Computing - Learning Path

Comprehensive Learning Path

This structured quantum learning path introduces students to the foundational concepts, phenomena, and practical implications of quantum computing. Each module deepens understanding, progressing from classical physics to advanced quantum algorithms and real-world applications.

Learning Path Modules

  • Module 1: Classical vs Quantum Thinking
    • Limitations of classical computing
    • Conceptual leap into quantum paradigms
    • Key contrasts: bits vs qubits, determinism vs probability
  • Module 2: The Language of Quantum Physics
    • Superposition, entanglement, and interference
    • Quantum states, measurement, and probability amplitudes
    • Basic linear algebra and Dirac notation
  • Module 3: Building Qubits
    • Quantum hardware: superconducting circuits, ion traps
    • How qubits are created, maintained, and manipulated
    • Quantum decoherence and error rates
  • Module 4: Quantum Gates and Circuits
    • Single- and multi-qubit gates
    • Quantum circuit diagrams
    • Building simple algorithms using basic gates
  • Module 5: Algorithms That Break and Build
    • Deutsch-Jozsa and Grover’s algorithm
    • Shor’s algorithm and its implications for encryption
    • Quantum speedups and their real-world impact
  • Module 6: Quantum Error Correction
    • Why quantum errors are different from classical ones
    • Introduction to stabilizer codes and surface codes
    • Challenges in fault-tolerant quantum computing
  • Module 7: Quantum Programming
    • Introduction to Qiskit and quantum SDKs
    • Writing and simulating quantum programs
    • Hands-on with quantum circuit simulators
  • Module 8: Real-World Applications
    • Quantum cryptography and communication
    • Quantum machine learning and optimization
    • Impact on finance, healthcare, and materials science
